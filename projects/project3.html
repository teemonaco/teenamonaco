Research Insights for Video Platform

Research uncovered that users frequently left the platform to watch videos on third-party sites. To address this, I designed a user-friendly video platform that retains users and enhances engagement.

User Behavior Insights

User data revealed a strong preference for drag-and-drop playlists, personalized content, and easy-to-use interfaces.

Competitor Analysis

We analyzed competitors like YouTube and Vimeo to identify features that could enhance user engagement on the Scrapbook platform.

Why Integrate an Inline Video Platform?

Key data points supporting the integration of an inline video platform for free education:

45%

YouTube takes a 45% revenue share from ads displayed on educational videos.

48%

Nearly 48% of users who leave to watch videos on YouTube do not immediately return to the original site.

120%

Sites integrating video content see a 120% increase in user time spent on the platform.

Personas for SBCTV Users

Sarah, 35 - Livestream Shopper Crafter

Goals: Watches live tutorials and wants to purchase supplies directly from the video platform while watching.

Frustrations: Disrupted flow when switching between video and shopping platforms.

Needs: Real-time shopping integration with the video stream and notifications for product deals.

Evelyn, 62 - Organized Crafter

Goals: Prefers an all-in-one platform where she can save videos, link projects, and share content.

Frustrations: Juggling between multiple platforms to find and save content.

Needs: A platform where she can organize her content and return to it easily.

Design Iterations

Several design iterations were made based on feedback from users, internal teams, and research to optimize the user experience.

Initial Playlist Design

The first version focused on drag-and-drop functionality for playlist creation. Users responded positively to the simplicity but wanted more customization options.

Refined Playlist Design

The refined version introduced personalized playlist suggestions, making it easier for users to find relevant content and create playlists tailored to their interests.

Prototypes

High-fidelity prototypes were created in Figma to simulate the user experience and were tested with real users for feedback.

Mobile Prototype

The mobile prototype ensured responsiveness and seamless drag-and-drop functionality, optimized for smaller screens.

Desktop Prototype

The desktop version incorporated advanced features like customizable playlists and content suggestions, driving higher user engagement.

Final Product: SBCTV

The final platform features a seamless drag-and-drop playlist functionality, live streaming capabilities, and personalized video recommendations, resulting in significantly improved user retention and engagement.

Live Streaming Integration

Seamless integration of live streams, allowing users to watch and engage with content directly on the platform.

Personalized Playlists

The platform allows users to easily create, save, and reorder playlists based on their favorite videos and content categories.
